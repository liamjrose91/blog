Risks that could hinder didgital projects


There are a multitude of risks involved with any type of work that is conducted in an online environment, data can become corrupted, a server night go down or your security could be compromised, there are steps that can be taken to soften the blow of such setbacks, like backups for example. Digital projects can fail due to all sorts of reasons, it could be that the team had elaborate and intricate ideas for their project but were unable to implement it due to lack of technical knowledge, lack of resources such as time or money, or maybe integral staff members left a gaping hole in your skill sets, it is always wise to sum up all the variables to mitigate these potential issues and constantly review project status and adjust accordingly.

The Eu general data protection regulation is the most important change to data privacy laws in 20 years, replacing the data protection directive 95/46/Ec, it outlines how personal data ought to be handled by any company or organisation that holds it, those in violation. Of the new rules will fave hearty fines. GDPR was put forward to unify the rules across Europe, to give people more say and transparency in how or when their data is used and to reshape how companies and organisations across the region approach privacy, one of the requirements was the ability for the user to download the information the organisation has regarding them.
